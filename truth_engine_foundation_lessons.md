# Truth Engine Foundation Lessons

Purpose: preserve only source-backed lessons that should shape architecture, stage gates, and agent guardrails.

## Core Lessons (Keep)

1. Treat startups as a growth system, not an idea system. Optimize for weekly learning and growth, not polished narratives. [S1][S2]
2. Start from painful, real problems (preferably observed directly), not "sitcom" ideas that sound plausible but lack urgency. [S2]
3. Early traction is manually manufactured: recruit users directly, do unscalable onboarding, and learn from high-touch use. [S3]
4. Scraped signals are lead generation, not validation. Validation requires customer discovery interviews and experiments with actionable metrics. [S4][S5][S6]
5. Distribution is a first-class constraint from day zero; every candidate needs a concrete first-channel thesis. [S3][S7]
6. PMF path matters (Hair on Fire vs Hard Fact vs Future Vision). Strategy, speed, and proof standards must adapt by path. [S8]
7. Inertia and switching friction kill otherwise good ideas; scoring must explicitly penalize behavior-change difficulty. [S8]
8. Build wedges, not full products. Win by entering upstream workflow, integrating deeply, and earning data position over time. [S9]
9. Willingness to pay beats attention. Commitment evidence (design partners, paid pilots, signed scope) outranks vanity signups. [S5][S8][S10]
10. Founder-market fit and "right to exist" matter: why this team, why now, why this market, and what unique wedge. [S8][S11]
11. Map the idea maze before committing: prior attempts, incumbents, changed assumptions, and likely dead ends. [S12]
12. Use explicit go/no-go deadlines and kill criteria to avoid endless iteration and budget bleed. [S5][S7][S8]

## Non-Negotiable Guardrails for This Project

- No evidence, no claim: decision-impacting claims require a source, quote, or experiment result.
- No channel, no candidate: if first 20 conversations are not reachable, kill or pause.
- No commitment, no build: no code-generation stage until commitment threshold is met.
- No open-ended spend: every candidate has stage budgets (compute, human time, outreach, paid spend).
- Always log disconfirming evidence: each candidate must store what would prove it wrong.

## Implications for Agent Design

- The orchestrator is a policy engine (state machine + budget enforcer), not a text coordinator.
- The evidence ledger is the core asset; agents write structured facts, not free-form opinions.
- Scoring must include distribution reachability, purchasing power, urgency, and switching friction.
- Validator agents should prioritize interviews and commitment experiments over social posting metrics.
- Skeptic/red-team agents must challenge assumptions and request missing evidence before advancement.
